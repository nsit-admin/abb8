Red Cross Standards of Conduct for Independent Contractors and Consultants

This workplace code of conduct (the “Code”) contains principles and conduct to standards which suppliers,contractors and consultants must adhere as they provide goods and services to the American Red Cross (the “Red Cross”).

Suppliers are responsible for ensuring:

1.this Code is shared with all individuals assigned to perform work on behalf of Red Cross,

2.any work performed for Red Cross is provided in full compliance with this code, all applicable laws and regulations and is accordance with the highest standards of ethical business conduct, and

3.any work performed for Red Cross is provided by trained individuals with the skills, expertise, and certifications necessary to complete the job in a safe and compliant manner.

FAILURE TO COMPLY WITH THESE OBLIGATIONS MAY RESULT IN TERMINATION OF SERVICES AND/OR CONTRACT.

Code of Business Ethics and Conduct

The Red Cross expects all contractors to meet the highest standard of professional and ethical conduct and at all times to conduct themselves in a positive manner that promotes the best interests of Red Cross. Conduct that interferes with business operations, discredits Red Cross, is offensive to clients or colleagues, is unethical, or violates this Code of Business Ethics and Conduct will not be tolerated.

1.Fundamental Principles of the International Red Cross Movement

Contractors and consultants are expected to not impede Red Cross from adhering to the Fundamental Principles of the International Red Cross Movement: humanity, impartiality, neutrality, independence, voluntary service, unity, and universality.

2.Code of Business Ethics and Conduct

The American Red Cross is a not-for-profit charitable organization dedicated to providing services to those in need. The Red Cross has traditionally demanded and received the highest ethical performance from its staff, contractors, consultants and suppliers. In an effort to maintain the high standard of conduct expected and deserved by the American public and to enable the organization to continue to offer its services, the American Red Cross operates under the Code of Business Ethics and Conduct outlined below. All contractors and consultants shall meet the following standards of conduct:

Compliance Requirements.All contractors and consultants are required to comply with applicable federal, state and local laws and regulations.

Actions Prohibited by the Code of Business Ethics and Conduct.No Contractoror consultant shall engage in the following actions:

a.Personal Use.Authorize the use of or use for the benefit or advantage of any person, the name, emblem, endorsement, services or property of Red Cross, except in conformance with Red Cross policy.

b.Financial Advantage. Accept or seek on behalf of or any other person, any financial advantage or gain of other than nominal value offered as a result of the contractors or consultants affiliation with Red Cross.

c.Red Cross Affiliation. Publicly use any Red Cross affiliation in connection with the promotion of partisan politics, religious matters or positions on any issue not in conformity with the official position of Red Cross.

d.Confidentiality. Disclose any confidential Red Cross information that is available solely as a result of a contractors or consultants’ affiliation with Red Cross to any person not authorized to receive such information, or use to the disadvantage of Red Cross any such confidential information, without the express authorization of Red Cross.

e.Improper Influence. Knowingly take any action or make any statement intended to influence the conduct of Red Cross in such a way as to confer any financial benefit on any person, corporation or entity in which the individual has a significant interest or affiliation.

f.Conflict of Interest. Operate or act in a manner that creates a conflict or appears to create a conflict with the interests of the American Red Cross and any organization in which the individual has a personal, business or financial interest.

g.Retaliation. Retaliate against any staff member, contractor, consultant or supplier who seeks advice from, raises a concern with or makes a complaint to a supervisor or other member of management, Red Cross ombudsman, the Concern Connection Line, the Biomedical Regulatory Hotline or any other whistleblower program, about fraud, waste, abuse, policy violations, discrimination, illegal conduct, unethical conduct, unsafe conduct or any other misconduct by the organization, its staff, contractors, consultants or suppliers.

h.Contrary to the Best Interest of Red Cross. Operate or act in any manner that is contrary to the best interest of Red Cross

Workplace Safety Policy

It is the policy of Red Cross to provide a safe and healthy work environment for all staff, contractors, consultants, and suppliers. While laws and regulations require workplace safety management programs it is the greater ethical commitment to protect workers, donors, and the public we serve that drives our commitment to workplace safety. Harassment-Free Work Environment

The Red Cross is committed to a work environment free from unlawful harassment where everyone is treated with respect and dignity. All interactions among staff, contractors and consultants while working, while on Red Cross premises, while traveling on Red Cross business or at Red Cross social functions will be free of unlawful harassment of any kind. Every contractor and consultant is responsible for complying with the letter and spirit of this policy, both by acting professionally and by using proper channels to stop conduct which may violate this policy. Contractors and consultants are responsible for reporting any concerns they may have regarding unlawful harassment to management and will not suffer retaliation for making their concerns known. Every report of perceived harassment will be fully investigated and corrective action will be taken where appropriate, in a timely and fair manner.

Prohibited Conduct -- The Red Cross has zero tolerance for unlawful harassment. Examples of conduct prohibited by this policy include, but are not limited to:

1.Verbal or physical conduct that harasses a staff member on the basis of a category protected by applicable federal, state or local law and that is sufficiently severe or pervasive as to create an intimidating, threatening, offensive, or hostile environment.

2.Sexual displays or publications, or other verbal or physical conduct, where a contractor or consultant is told either explicitly or implicitly that he or she must submit to the conduct to remain employed or where his or her reaction to the conduct is used as a basis for an employment decision, such as evaluation, raise, advancement, assigned duties, disciplinary action, or any other condition of employment or career development. Examples of prohibited verbal or physical conduct include:

a.unwelcome sexual advances,

b.stalking, dating violence, date rape, or sexual assault,

c.persisting with romantic advances despite an individual’s rejection of the advances,

d.requests for sexual favors, whether or not accompanied by promises or threats with regard to the employment relationship,

e.sexual jokes and innuendo; verbal abuse of a sexual nature; comments about an individual's body, sexual prowess, sexual activity, or sexual attractiveness,

f.leering, whistling, or touching; insulting or obscene comments, sounds, or gestures; displays of sexually suggestive objects, cartoons or pictures, and

g.words, actions or visual matter that demean or show hostility toward an individual or group because of race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, marital status, citizenship, status as a covered veteran or any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state or local law.

No contractor or consultant will be retaliated against for acting in good faith to report a possible violation of this policy or for assisting in the investigation of a possible violation. Violations of this policy will be dealt with appropriately and promptly. If, after investigating any claim of unlawful harassment, Red Cross concludes that a staff member, contractor or consultant has filed a claim in bad faith, has refused to cooperate in an investigation of a claim, or has provided false information regarding the claim, disciplinary action up to and including termination may be taken against the staff member, contractor or consultant.

The Red Cross has an organizational ombudsman designated as the neutral or impartial dispute resolution practitioner whose major function is to provide confidential and informal assistance to the many constituents with concerns or complaints about Red Cross.
